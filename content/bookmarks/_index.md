Things I actually use and keep coming back to. Repos, tools, references, and the occasional rabbit hole worth going down.

---

## Kubernetes

- [Kubernetes Official Docs](https://kubernetes.io/docs/home/) — The authoritative reference. Concepts, API docs, and tasks all in one place. I still find myself here regularly regardless of how long I've been working with it.

## AWS

- [AWS Well-Architected Framework](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/framework/welcome.html) — The five pillars every AWS architecture should be measured against. Useful both for reviews and for stress-testing your own design decisions.

## Courses

- [Jenkins Full Course](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLhW3qG5bs-L_ZCOA4zNPSoGbnVQ-rp_dG) — Solid end-to-end coverage of Jenkins for CI/CD. Good for getting up to speed or filling in gaps.
- [Prometheus Full Course](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLyBW7UHmEXgylLwxdVbrBQJ-fJ_jMvh8h) — Covers Prometheus from the ground up including alerting and Grafana integration. The one I recommend when people are getting started with observability.

## Terraform & IaC

- [awesome-tf](https://github.com/shuaibiyy/awesome-tf) — Curated list of Terraform resources, tools, and community projects. Good starting point when looking for modules, providers, or best practice patterns.
